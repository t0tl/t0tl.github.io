Exercise Inh.1

Assume that we have four classes: Person, Teacher, Student, and PhDStudent. Teacher and Student are both subclasses of Person. PhDStudent is a subclass of Student.

a. Which of the following assignments are legal, and why or why not?

Person p1 = new Student(); legal as student is a type of person
Person p2 = new PhDStudent(); legal as PhD student is a type of person
PhDStudent phd1 = new Student(); illegal as Student is not a type of PhD student. (Student is a superset but here labelled a subset)
Teacher t1 = new Person(); illegal as Person is not a subset of Teacher, the opposite is true.
Student s1 = new PhDStudent(); legal as PhDstudent inherits from student, it is a subset of student

b. Suppose that we have the following legal declarations and assignments:

Person p1 = new Person();
Person p2 = new Person();
PhDStudent phd1 = new PhDStudent();
Teacher t1 = new Teacher();
Student s1 = new Student();

Based on those just mentioned, which of the following assignments are legal, and why or why not?

s1 = p1; illegal as person is a superset of student and a superset cannot be assigned to a subset
s1 = p2; illegal as person is a superset of student and a superset cannot be assigned to a subset
p1 = s1; legal as student is a subset of person.
t1 = s1; illegal as student is mutually exclusive from teacher, hence cannot be assigned.
s1 = phd1; legal as PhDstudent is a subset of student.
phd1 = s1; illegal as student is a superset of PhDstudent

a. Argue the correctness of the algorithms using a loop invariant or proof by induction.

double expIterative(double x, int n) {
    double res = 1.0;

    for (int i = 0; i < n; i++) {
